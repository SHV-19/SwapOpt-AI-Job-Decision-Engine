SWAPOPT USER PREFERENCES
========================

Primary Goal:
Get hired into a strong analytics role with realistic H-1B sponsorship potential.

Work Authorization:
Candidate needs H-1B sponsorship.
This should heavily affect job priority.

Preferred Role Types:
- Data Analyst
- Business Data Analyst
- BI Analyst
- Reporting Analyst
- Customer Data Analyst
- Product Analyst
- Marketing Analyst
- Operations Analyst
- Supply Chain Analyst
- Analytics Engineer only if not too engineering-heavy

Highest Priority Roles:
- Full-time roles
- SQL + Power BI/Tableau + dashboards
- Snowflake/data warehouse roles
- Business-facing analytics
- Customer, marketing, product, operations, supply chain, retail, e-commerce analytics

Lower Priority Roles:
- Contract/temp roles
- Government roles
- Roles requiring 7+ years
- Senior/Lead/Principal roles unless requirements are flexible
- Highly niche domain roles
- Pure software engineering
- Heavy production data engineering
- ML research/scientist roles requiring advanced research background

Location Preferences:
Preferred:
- Remote USA
- Hybrid/onsite roles in strong sponsor companies
- Dallas/Fort Worth
- Phoenix/Arizona
- Texas
- Major tech/analytics markets if sponsorship is strong

Application Time Strategy:
Be time-sensitive.
The assistant should help avoid wasting time.

High Priority:
Apply/tailor when:
- role is full-time
- tools match strongly
- experience level is close
- employer likely sponsors
- candidate can defend the fit in interview

Medium Priority:
Quick apply when:
- role is possible but has gaps
- tailoring ROI is not high
- company is decent but sponsorship unclear

Low Priority:
Skip when:
- H-1B risk is high
- role is contract/temp
- seniority mismatch is large
- domain mismatch is large
- job requires unrelated certifications or industry-specific experience

Scoring Preference:
Do not give inflated match scores.
A realistic 75-85% can be excellent.
100% should almost never be used.
Focus more on hiring logic and time ROI than keyword overlap.

Preferred Output Tone:
Be direct.
Tell the user clearly:
- Apply today
- Tailor first
- Quick apply only
- Save for later
- Skip

Avoid vague advice.
